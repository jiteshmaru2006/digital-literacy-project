Cybercrime Type: UPI / Online Payment Fraud

UPI (Unified Payments Interface) fraud is one of the most common cybercrimes in India, 
especially among students and new digital payment users. In this type of fraud, criminals 
trick victims into sending money or sharing sensitive banking information through fake 
messages, calls, or payment requests.

A common scenario begins when a student posts an item for sale online or receives a message 
about a prize or cashback offer. The fraudster contacts the student pretending to be a buyer,
bank official, or customer support representative. They then send a UPI “collect request” or 
ask the victim to scan a QR code to “receive money.” Many victims mistakenly approve the 
request, believing they will receive funds. In reality, the approval transfers money from the 
victim’s account to the fraudster.

Fraudsters often target college students because they frequently use digital payments for food 
orders, travel, and online shopping. Students may also be less experienced in identifying fake 
payment requests or phishing messages.

The consequences can include immediate financial loss, unauthorized bank transactions, and 
exposureof personal information such as phone numbers and bank account details. In some cases, 
victims also face emotional stress and difficulty recovering the stolen money.
